\subsection{Generator-level signed shoving test in inclusive hadronic events at \texorpdfstring{$\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV}{sqrt(s)=200 GeV}}
\label{sec:qq200-signed-generator-test}

As a cross-check distinct from the hadronic \(W^+W^-\) study, we generated inclusive
hadronic \(e^+e^- \to \gamma^*/Z \to q\bar{q}\) events at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV with
Pythia8.  The samples use the same charged-particle selection as the cumulant analysis,
\(p_T>0.4\) GeV, and the same \(N_{\mathrm{trk}}^{\mathrm{offline}}\) bins.  Three
generator settings are compared: no shoving, nominal Gleipnir shoving, and enhanced
Gleipnir shoving with twice the nominal repulsion factor.

The signed observable is the underlying two-particle correlator
\[
  c_2\{2\}=\left<\cos 2(\phi_i-\phi_j)\right>,
\]
rather than only \(v_2\{2\}=\sqrt{c_2\{2\}}\).  The signed response is evaluated as
\[
  \Delta c_2 = c_2^{\mathrm{shoving}} - c_2^{\mathrm{no~shoving}},
\]
for inclusive pairs, rapidity-gap pairs, and two-subevent pairs.  The long-range
thrust-axis coefficient \(V_{2\Delta}\) is also extracted from pairs with
\(1.6<|\Delta\eta|<3.2\), together with the signed proxy
\(\mathrm{sign}(V_{2\Delta})\sqrt{|V_{2\Delta}|}\).

The generated trees contain final-state saved particles and their production vertices,
but not a complete event record, string-piece geometry, or mother/daughter links.  We
therefore do not define a geometry-plane signed \(v_2\) for this inclusive sample.
The signed \(c_2\) and \(V_{2\Delta}\) tests preserve the sign of the two-particle
response, but they are not direct spatial-geometry sign tests.

The completed 5M-sample comparison gives a positive signed thrust-axis shoving
response in the representative bins.  In the
\(N_{\mathrm{trk}}^{\mathrm{offline}}=[30,35)\) bin, the inclusive thrust-axis
response is \(\Delta c_2=+0.00701\) for nominal shoving and \(+0.01290\) for
enhanced shoving.  With \(|\Delta\eta|>1.6\), the corresponding values are
\(+0.00266\) and \(+0.00476\).  For the two-subevent selection
\(\eta<-0.8\) versus \(\eta>0.8\), the responses are \(+0.00166\) and
\(+0.00255\).  The long-range thrust-axis coefficient also increases, with
\(\Delta V_{2\Delta}=+0.00382\) for nominal shoving and \(+0.00698\) for
enhanced shoving in the same multiplicity bin.  Thus the inclusive
\(q\bar{q}\) 200 GeV generator-level study shows a sign-preserving positive
two-particle shoving response in these observables, with enhanced shoving larger
than nominal shoving.
